# Legal and Safety

This project documents legitimate, regulator-compliant ways to get more out of your drone. Before you do anything in this repository, understand your local rules.

## What we explicitly will NOT help with

- Removing No-Fly Zones (NFZ / Geofencing)
- Bypassing Remote ID requirements
- Operating beyond regulatory altitude (typically 120m / 400ft)
- Operating beyond visual line of sight (BVLOS) without a waiver
- Exceeding legal transmit power for your region
- Flying over crowds, near airports, or in restricted airspace
- Anything that disables manufacturer safety features

These restrictions exist because drones share the sky with manned aircraft and operate over people. We are not interested in publishing material that increases the risk of fatal incidents.

## Per-country regulatory references

Always check the most recent rules — these change frequently.

### 🇺🇸 United States

- **Authority:** [Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)](https://www.faa.gov/uas)
- **Recreational:** Pass [TRUST](https://www.faa.gov/uas/recreational_flyers/knowledge_test_updates) (free, online).
- **Commercial:** Part 107 license.
- **Remote ID:** Required as of September 2023 for almost all drones.
- **Altitude:** 400 ft AGL.
- **Registration:** Required for drones over 250g via [FAA DroneZone](https://faadronezone.faa.gov).
- **Authorization tool:** [B4UFLY](https://b4ufly.aloft.ai/) for airspace status.

### 🇪🇺 European Union (EASA)

- **Authority:** [EASA](https://www.easa.europa.eu/en/domains/civil-drones)
- **Categories:** Open / Specific / Certified.
- **Open category subcategories:** A1 (over people), A2 (close to people), A3 (far from people).
- **Registration:** Required in your country of residence.
- **Pilot competency:** A1/A3 online test, A2 additional theoretical exam.
- **Class identification labels (C0–C4):** New drones must carry these.
- **Altitude:** 120m AGL.

### 🇬🇧 United Kingdom

- **Authority:** [Civil Aviation Authority (CAA)](https://register-drones.caa.co.uk/)
- **Operator ID and Flyer ID** required.
- **Categories:** Match EASA structure.
- **Altitude:** 120m AGL.

### 🇮🇱 Israel

- **Authority:** [רת״א — רשות התעופה האזרחית (CAAI)](https://caa.gov.il)
- **Hobbyist flight:** Up to 50m AGL in non-restricted areas, line of sight, daytime only without specific permission.
- **Commercial flight:** Requires operator certification and specific permit per flight class.
- **No-fly zones:** Most of central Israel is restricted airspace. Use the official map app.
- **Drone weight limits:** Different rules above and below 4kg.

### 🇨🇦 Canada

- **Authority:** [Transport Canada](https://tc.canada.ca/en/aviation/drone-safety)
- **Categories:** Basic / Advanced.
- **Pilot certificate:** Required for both categories.
- **Registration:** Required for drones 250g–25kg.

### 🇦🇺 Australia

- **Authority:** [CASA](https://www.casa.gov.au/drones)
- **Sub-2kg recreational:** No license, but standard operating conditions apply.
- **Commercial or 2kg+:** Remote pilot license + operator certificate.
- **Altitude:** 120m AGL.

### 🇯🇵 Japan

- **Authority:** [MLIT](https://www.mlit.go.jp/koku/koku_tk10_000003.html)
- **Registration required** for drones 100g+.
- **Remote ID required.**
- **No-fly zones:** Tokyo and other dense urban areas heavily restricted.

### 🇨🇳 China

- **Authority:** CAAC.
- **Real-name registration required** for drones 250g+.
- **No-fly zones:** Strictly enforced; DJI provides geofencing aligned with these.

## Universal best practices

1. **Pre-flight checklist** every flight: battery, props, calibration, weather, NOTAMs.
2. **Visual line of sight** at all times unless you have a BVLOS waiver.
3. **Max altitude** matches your local rule, not what the drone can do.
4. **Don't fly over people** unless your category permits.
5. **Don't fly over critical infrastructure**: airports, prisons, military bases, power plants.
6. **Respect privacy.** Don't film private property without consent.
7. **Insurance.** Hobbyist liability is cheap and recommended; commercial pilots usually require it.
8. **Log every flight.** Use Airdata or DJI's built-in logs.

## When something goes wrong

- **Loss of control:** RTH (Return to Home). Verify RTH altitude is set high enough to clear obstacles.
- **Battery warning:** Land immediately. Don't try to push back home.
- **Lost signal:** Most DJI drones auto-RTH after a few seconds.
- **Crash:** Do not power on the battery if damaged. Do not attempt to fly again until inspected.
- **Near-miss with manned aircraft:** Report it. In the US, file an [ASRS report](https://asrs.arc.nasa.gov/). In Israel, contact רת\"א.
